# The Home Kitchen Masterclass
### A 12-week plant-based skills curriculum for Michael & Masa

---

## The idea behind this

This is built on how MasterChef contestants actually develop the ability to "pull a soufflé out of thin air" — which, it turns out, is far less about innate talent than about a handful of deliberate habits:

1. **Ratios over recipes.** Once you've internalised that a vinaigrette is 3:1, or that pasta dough has a fixed backbone, you stop needing recipes and start *composing*. Contestants keep notebooks of memorised ratios precisely because they can't look anything up mid-challenge.
2. **Drill your weak spots, not your hits.** The most useful prep stories are about people attacking the things they *avoided* — not cooking what they were already good at.
3. **Repetition, not novelty.** One technique, done three or four times in a week until it's muscle memory — the show's weekend-class model — beats cooking a different impressive thing every night.
4. **Invent under constraint.** Regular "mystery box" cooks with no recipe and a timer build the on-the-spot composition that looks like magic on screen.
5. **Cook for an audience and get honest feedback.**

Masa already lives in the part most people spend years building — flavour, structure, iteration. So this curriculum isn't about teaching recipe-following. It's about **technical range, working without a net, and the quiet confidence that comes from owning your fundamentals.** Where a week feels easy for her, the job is to push the "confidence stretch" harder.

---

## How to use it

- **Three sessions a week** is the target. One ratio session, one technique-drill session, one invention session. Adjust to your life — consistency beats volume.
- **Keep a shared notebook** (paper or a note on your phone). Every ratio you internalise goes in it. By week 12 this is your personal cookbook-in-your-head.
- **Repeat, don't rush.** If a technique isn't muscle memory by the end of its week, carry it forward. The weeks are a spine, not a deadline.
- **Photograph every invention cook.** It's how you'll see progress, and it doubles as Heartful Table material if you want it to.
- **Rate each invention cook out of 10 and write one sentence on what you'd change.** That single sentence is where the learning compounds.

Each week has the same shape: **Focus → Ratios to own → Technique drills → Invention challenge → Confidence stretch.**

# Phase 1 — Foundations & flavour (Weeks 1–4)

The unglamorous bedrock. Get these solid and everything downstream gets easier.

## Week 1 — Knife skills & heat control

**Focus:** Speed, consistency, and safety with a knife; understanding what high vs. low heat actually does.

**Ratios to own:**
- Salt as a starting point: ~1% of the weight of the food is a reliable baseline to season *toward* (e.g. 5g salt per 500g). Taste up from there.

**Technique drills:**
- The four core cuts: dice, julienne, chiffonade, brunoise. Work through a kilo of onions, carrots and herbs across the week. Time yourself; aim for *even*, then *fast*.
- Cook the same vegetable (mushrooms work beautifully) three ways: screaming-hot sear, gentle sweat, slow caramelisation. Notice how heat is the variable, not the ingredient.

**Invention challenge:** A meal built from one vegetable cut three different ways.

**Confidence stretch:** Cook one full dinner without looking at your hands more than necessary — eyes on the pan, trusting the knife.

---

## Week 2 — Emulsions & dressings (the vegan superpower)

**Focus:** Making things creamy and cohesive without dairy or egg. This is the single most confidence-building plant-based skill, because it's where most people assume they "need" animal products.

**Ratios to own:**
- **Vinaigrette: 3 parts oil : 1 part acid**, plus salt + an emulsifier (mustard or a little aquafaba). Memorise this forever.
- **Aquafaba mayo:** aquafaba (chickpea brine) as the egg-replacement base, then drizzle oil *slowly* while blending until it thickens. Ratio is feel-based: keep adding oil until it holds.
- **Cashew cream:** roughly 1 part soaked cashews : 1 part water, blended smooth; less water = thicker.

**Technique drills:**
- Make a vinaigrette by feel (no measuring) and check yourself against the 3:1.
- Make one broken sauce *on purpose*, then bring it back — learning to rescue an emulsion kills the fear of it.

**Invention challenge:** Build a meal around three different sauces from the same base technique (one vinaigrette, one emulsified, one cashew-based).

**Confidence stretch:** A composed salad good enough to serve to guests, dressed three ways on the plate.

---

## Week 3 — Stocks, bases & layering flavour

**Focus:** Why restaurant food tastes "deeper." Building umami and savouriness from plants — the thing vegan cooking is most often (unfairly) accused of lacking.

**Ratios to own:**
- **Soffritto / mirepoix:** roughly 2 parts onion : 1 part carrot : 1 part celery — the flavour base under a huge share of savoury cooking.
- A working vegetable stock you can make without a recipe.

**Technique drills:**
- The umami toolkit: practise deploying miso, soy/tamari, nutritional yeast, dried mushrooms, tomato paste, smoked paprika, seaweed. Cook one simple broth and push it from "fine" to "deep" using these, tasting at each addition.
- Make a soffritto properly slow — 20+ minutes — and taste the difference against a rushed one.

**Invention challenge:** A soup or braise where the depth has to come entirely from technique and the umami toolkit, no shortcuts.

**Confidence stretch:** A two-component dish where a homemade stock is the star, not an afterthought.

---

## Week 4 — Sauces & the art of finishing

**Focus:** A dish is often "made" in the last two minutes. Pan sauces, reductions, and the roux family — veganised.

**Ratios to own:**
- **Roux:** equal weights fat (vegan butter or oil) and flour.
- **Béchamel:** ~30g vegan butter + 30g flour per 500ml plant milk for a medium sauce (the mother sauce behind mac & cheese, lasagne, gratins).
- **Reduction logic:** acid + fat + seasoning, cooked down until it coats the back of a spoon.

**Technique drills:**
- Make a béchamel three times until it's lump-free without thinking.
- Build a pan sauce from fond (the browned bits) after searing mushrooms or tofu.

**Invention challenge:** One protein/vegetable element, elevated by a sauce built from scratch in the same pan.

**Confidence stretch:** A vegan gratin or lasagne carried entirely by a confident béchamel.

---

# Phase 2 — Plant proteins & texture (Weeks 5–8)

This is the heart of vegan technique and where the most range lives.

## Week 5 — Tofu mastery

**Focus:** Turning a blank canvas into something with crust, chew, and flavour. Tofu is the egg of vegan cooking — endlessly versatile once you stop being afraid of it.

**Ratios to own:**
- **Crisping coating:** a light dusting of cornstarch is the difference between soggy and shatteringly crisp.
- **Marinade balance:** salty (soy) + sweet + acid + aromatic, in rough balance — taste and adjust.

**Technique drills:**
- Press, then cook the same block four ways across the week: pan-crisped, baked, silken-blended (into a sauce or mousse), and braised. One ingredient, four textures.
- Get a genuinely crisp crust without deep-frying.

**Invention challenge:** A meal where tofu appears twice in two completely different textures.

**Confidence stretch:** Silken tofu as the base of a savoury sauce *and* a sweet dessert in the same week — proving how far one ingredient stretches.

---

## Week 6 — Tempeh, legumes & beans from scratch

**Focus:** The protein workhorses, treated with intention rather than dumped from a can.

**Ratios to own:**
- **Cooking dried beans:** the principle of cooking in well-salted, aromatic liquid (not water alone) — pot likker as a bonus ingredient.
- **Tempeh:** steam first to mellow bitterness, *then* marinate and cook.

**Technique drills:**
- Cook one pot of dried beans from scratch and taste them against canned — this alone changes how you cook.
- Crumble and crisp tempeh as a "mince" replacement; braise it as a "chop."

**Invention challenge:** A bean or lentil dish where the beans are the hero, not a filler — texture and seasoning carrying it.

**Confidence stretch:** A composed plate built around tempeh as the centrepiece, treated like you'd treat a steak.

---

## Week 7 — Seitan, mushrooms & "meaty" texture

**Focus:** The most technically impressive corner of vegan cooking — building chew and structure from wheat gluten, and coaxing depth from fungi.

**Ratios to own:**
- **Seitan base:** vital wheat gluten + liquid + seasoning, kneaded for elasticity then simmered/steamed. The kneading and resting *is* the technique.

**Technique drills:**
- Make seitan once from scratch — it's the closest plant-based equivalent to "making meat," and nailing it is a real confidence milestone.
- Treat king oyster or lion's mane mushrooms as a protein: scoring, pressing, searing hard.

**Invention challenge:** A "centre of the plate" dish where the protein is something you built, not bought.

**Confidence stretch:** A dish you'd genuinely serve to a sceptical omnivore and expect to win them over.

---

## Week 8 — The vegan egg toolkit

**Focus:** Demystifying the function eggs play — binding, leavening, emulsifying, aerating — and replacing each one deliberately. This is the week that unlocks baking and desserts later.

**Ratios to own:**
- **Flax/chia egg (binding):** 1 tbsp ground flax + 3 tbsp water = 1 egg.
- **Aquafaba (aerating/whites):** ~3 tbsp aquafaba ≈ 1 egg white; whips to peaks with a pinch of cream of tartar.

**Technique drills:**
- Whip aquafaba to stiff peaks — actually stiff, holds-over-your-head stiff. Most people stop too early. Do it until you know what "stiff" looks like.
- Use the same aquafaba whipped into a savoury foam *and* folded for lift.

**Invention challenge:** One dish that depends on a vegan egg replacement working correctly — and would fail if it didn't.

**Confidence stretch:** A stable aquafaba meringue (this is the gateway to Week 11's desserts).

---

# Phase 3 — Advanced technique & composition (Weeks 9–12)

Putting it together, working under pressure, and plating like it matters.

## Week 9 — Doughs & fresh pasta

**Focus:** Working dough by feel — the most "I made this from nothing" skill there is.

**Ratios to own:**
- **Eggless fresh pasta:** roughly 2 parts semolina : 1 part water by weight (100g semolina : ~50g water), adjusted to a smooth, springy dough. Feel beats the scale here.
- **Dough hydration as a dial:** wetter = softer, drier = chewier.

**Technique drills:**
- Make, rest, roll and cut fresh pasta by hand. Repeat until the dough *tells* you when it's right.
- One filled shape (ravioli or tortellini) using a Week-5 or Week-7 filling.

**Invention challenge:** A pasta dish where both the pasta and the sauce are made from scratch in one session.

**Confidence stretch:** Hand-rolled pasta thin enough to see your hand through it.

---

## Week 10 — Bread & lamination

**Focus:** Fermentation and the discipline of dough that takes time. Bread is almost entirely vegan by default — this is pure technique.

**Ratios to own:**
- **Baker's percentages:** flour 100%, water ~65%, salt ~2%, yeast ~1%. Internalising this lets you scale any bread up or down by instinct.

**Technique drills:**
- Bake one straightforward loaf, paying attention to gluten development and proof — under-proofed vs. over-proofed is a feel you can only learn by doing.
- Attempt a simple laminated dough (rough puff with vegan butter) for the confidence hit.

**Invention challenge:** A meal built around bread you baked that day.

**Confidence stretch:** A laminated pastry — savoury or sweet — with visible layers.

---

## Week 11 — Desserts & sugar work

**Focus:** The classic "I could never" frontier. Vegan desserts lean on Weeks 2 and 8 — emulsions and the egg toolkit — so you arrive ready.

**Ratios to own:**
- **Pastry cream / custard:** ~40g cornstarch + 50–80g sugar per 500ml plant milk, cooked until thick; a pinch of turmeric for colour, vanilla to finish.
- **Caramel:** sugar melted (with a splash of water), then plant cream whisked in off the heat for a sauce.

**Technique drills:**
- Make a smooth, lump-free vegan custard/pastry cream.
- Take aquafaba meringue from Week 8 all the way to a baked pavlova or macaron shells.
- Make caramel without burning it — then make it again, pushing it a shade darker.

**Invention challenge:** A plated dessert with three distinct components (a cream, a crisp/base, a sauce or fruit element).

**Confidence stretch:** A restaurant-style plated dessert you'd be proud to put in front of guests.

---

## Week 12 — The MasterChef week

**Focus:** Everything, under pressure. This is your "pressure test" and "invention test" rolled into a finale.

**The challenge (do it together, then critique each other):**
1. **Mystery box (90 minutes).** Each of you cooks a complete dish from a shared box of ingredients neither of you chose — no recipes, no phones. The other sets the box.
2. **Signature dish (timed).** Each of you plates a single dish that represents who you are as a cook, in under a set time, drawing on everything from the past 11 weeks.
3. **Plate, photograph, and judge honestly.** Score each other out of 10 on flavour, technique, and presentation, with one specific note each.

**Confidence stretch:** A three-course vegan menu cooked for guests — your own small MasterChef finale, and frankly a great Huon Happy Hour or Heartful Table moment.

---

## Where to point the camera (for Masa specifically)

The weeks Masa will likely find easy — flavour balance, seasoning, composition — are where her existing recipe-development instincts already shine. The genuine growth edges for a confident recipe developer tend to be:

- **Working without measuring** (Weeks 2, 9) — trusting feel over the scale.
- **High-heat, high-stakes technique** (seitan, lamination, sugar work, meringue) — where the margin for error is real and nailing it is the confidence unlock.
- **Speed under a clock** (Week 12) — composition is one thing; composition in 90 minutes with no recipe is another.

Lean the stretches there.

---

## Tracking progress

Keep the shared notebook updated with every ratio you internalise. By Week 12 you should be able to make — without a recipe — a vinaigrette, a béchamel, fresh pasta, a custard, bread, and an aquafaba meringue. That's a genuinely formidable foundation, and it's the same one the contestants you admire are quietly running on.

Most of all: cook often, repeat deliberately, and feed people. That's the whole secret.
